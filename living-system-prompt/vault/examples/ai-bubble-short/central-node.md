## Prior statement

A structured short against the AI bubble using options with capped maximum loss.
The thesis: valuations of AI-exposed equities (semiconductors, hyperscalers, software)
have exceeded fundamentals, sustained by coordinated speculative beliefs fragile to
earnings disappointments, rising real rates, or capex ROI disappointment.

Preferred instruments: debit put spreads (primary), long puts (crash layer).
Naked shorts avoided due to borrow costs, squeeze risk, and margin volatility.

## Key assumptions

1. AI capex growth continues to outpace realized productivity gains.
2. Borrow fees on high-conviction names remain below 10% annually.
3. IV remains elevated enough to make put spreads cost-efficient.
4. The bubble deflates over a 4–16 week horizon per position.

## Falsification conditions

- Sustained earnings beats with genuine AI revenue growth across the universe.
- IV collapse below historical norms (spreads become uneconomical).
- Regulatory intervention restricting short selling in target names.
- Macro regime shift to sustained risk-on with falling real rates.
